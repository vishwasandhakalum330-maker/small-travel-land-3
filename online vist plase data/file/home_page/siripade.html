Adams Peak (Sri Pada) – Central Province

Geography & Location

Adams Peak rises to 2,243 meters in the central highlands near Maskeliya. It is part of a more extensive mountainous region accessible from towns like Nuwara Eliya and Ratnapura.

History & Cultural Significance

Adams Peak is revered by multiple faiths because of the sacred footprint imprint at its summit. Buddhists call it Sri Pada, Hindus link it to Shiva, and other faiths attribute the print to Adam or St. Thomas. The mountain has been a pilgrimage site for centuries.

Main Attractions & Highlights

The summit footprint shrine and the pilgrimage stairway.

Spectacular sunrise views and the “shadow of the peak” phenomenon.

Pilgrimage atmosphere with hundreds to thousands of climbers during season.

Activities & Experiences

Night climb to reach the summit for dawn — a common pilgrim practice.

Visiting smaller shrines and tea plantations in the surrounding area.

Observing local traditions and the communal nature of the climb during season.

Travel Tips

Best Season: December–May (official pilgrimage season).

Preparation: strong torch/headlamp, warm clothing, snacks, and water.

Fitness: the climb can take 2–4 hours depending on starting point and pace.

Book local accommodation in advance during peak pilgrimage days.
