Yala National Park – Southern Provincec

Geography & Location

Located in the southeastern part of Sri Lanka, spanning Hambantota and Monaragala Districts, Yala covers nearly 1,000 square kilometers. It is 300 km from Colombo and accessible via road (about 6–7 hours).

History & Cultural Significance

Yala was designated a wildlife sanctuary in 1900 and later a national park in 1938. It has both natural and cultural importance, as ancient Buddhist ruins, temples, and stupas are scattered within its borders.

Main Attractions & Highlights

Leopards: Yala has the highest density of leopards in the world.

Elephants: Frequently seen during safaris.

Birdlife: Over 200 bird species, including endemic ones.

Scenery: Diverse landscapes of scrub jungle, lagoons, beaches, and rocky outcrops.

Sithulpawwa Temple: Ancient Buddhist site inside the park.

Activities & Experiences

Jeep safari tours at dawn or dusk.

Wildlife photography, especially of leopards.

Visit the nearby Kataragama Temple, a multi-religious pilgrimage site.

Travel Tips

Best Season: February–July (dry season, better visibility of animals).

Entrance Fee: Around USD 25–30 per person, plus jeep hire.

Safaris: Book in advance with a registered guide.

* Carry water, hats, and sunscreen — it gets very hot.
