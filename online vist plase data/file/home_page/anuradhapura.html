Anuradhapura – North Central Province

Geography & Location

Anuradhapura is in the North Central Province, approximately 205 km from Colombo and part of Sri Lanka’s “cultural triangle” of ancient capitals.

History & Cultural Significance

Founded in the 4th century BC, Anuradhapura was the island’s first major capital and a center for Theravada Buddhism. It remained politically and religiously important for more than a millennium. The city’s ruins include stupas, monasteries, and irrigation works that reflect advanced ancient engineering and devotional life.

Main Attractions & Highlights

Sri Maha Bodhi (sacred fig tree grown from the original Bodhi tree).

Ruwanwelisaya and Jetavanaramaya stupas (massive brick structures).

Ancient reservoir systems like Tissa Wewa and Nuwara Wewa

* Monastic complexes such as Thuparamaya and Abhayagiri.

Activities & Experiences

TRent a bicycle to explore the expansive archaeological park.

Observe devout pilgrims performing rituals at dawn or dusk.

Visit the Archaeological Museum to contextualize the ruins.

Combine with a visit to Mihintale (nearby sacred mountain).

Travel Tips

Best Season: May–September (dry zone; less rain).

Dress respectfully when visiting religious sites (shoulders and knees covered).

Start early to avoid midday heat; many sites are spread out — cycling saves time.

There are entry tickets for the archaeological zone; keep them for re-entry.
