Jaffna – Northern Province

Geography & Location

Jaffna is at Sri Lanka’s northern tip, connected to the rest of the island by road and rail. It is the principal city of the Northern Province and a hub for exploring nearby islands and cultural sites.

History & Cultural Significance

Jaffna is the center of Tamil culture in Sri Lanka with deep Hindu traditions. After prolonged conflict, the city has been rebuilding and re-opening to tourism, showcasing a resilient heritage, unique cuisine, and historical architecture.

Main Attractions & Highlights

Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil, an important Hindu temple with vibrant festivals.

Jaffna Fort, built by the Portuguese and expanded by the Dutch.

Jaffna Public Library, a symbol of cultural recovery.

Nearby islands such as Delft (famous for wild ponies and colonial ruins) and Karaitivu.

Activities & Experiences

Sampling Jaffna cuisine — crab curries, coconut sambol, and jaggery sweets.

Island-hopping to Delft and Nainativu (Buddhist and Hindu pilgrimage sites).

Exploring markets for local palm sugar, chilies, and handicrafts.

Travel Tips

Best Season: May–September (northern dry season)

Respect religious customs when visiting temples (cover shoulders and legs)./p>

Public transport options are improving, but hiring a private driver is convenient for island tours.

Accommodation varies from guesthouses to small hotels; book ahead for festival periods.
